Prelab

ToF Sensor Address

The ToF sensor, VL53L1X, has a hardwired address. I chose to programmatically change the address of the sensor upon startup rather than enable two sensors separately through their shutdown pins. This was mostly because I didn't want the hassle of dealing with mutual exclusion. I figured that trying to read from two sensors with the same address at the same time would be a lot like dealing with locks for processes that cannot run simultaneously. In all honesty, I am just not that good at keeping track of multiple things, so for me, as the designer and user of this robot, it was easier to program a separate address for one of the sensors.

I understand that a downside is that if there is a power issue, the sensor address reverts to its default. I hope that is an unlikely event, or at least won't occur while I am using the robot, so I'm comfortable taking that risk.

ToF Sensor Placement on Robot and Possible Scenarios

During lecture the day that we discussed this, my friend and I decided that it would be smart to mount the sensors at the front and back of the car. To me this is kind of like having a reverse camera in a car, and a person driving it seeing forward.

In a scenario where the user wanted to navigate a series of obstacles in a classroom, or lab, the only visual data that matters is that the car can physically fit in that space. Therefore, a front-mounted sensor placed at the top rim of the car would guarantee that there is space in front of the car to move forward, and since the car is so low to the ground, it is very unlikely that anything below the sensor will be blocking the car's path. It doesn't matter that we can't sense what's above the car's height, because it won't hit our car anyway.

The sensor in the back would be for maneuvering better. Just like some cars have a reverse camera, it would be helpful to have this raw data to know where it is possible to turn and move in reverse. It would also make it easier to track distances. Imagine a scenario in which the starting line of the car is in front of a wall and it has to make it to the other side of the room as fast as possible. Having a ToF sensor on the back would allow you to track how far you've traveled, and therefore how much more you have to go.
